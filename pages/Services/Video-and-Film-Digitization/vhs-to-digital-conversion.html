Tips to Prepare Your VHS Tapes for Digitizing

To make sure your VHS to digital conversion is as smooth and high-quality as possible, here are some important steps you can take before bringing your tapes to us:



1. Store Your Tapes Properly

Keep your VHS tapes away from extreme heat, moisture, and direct sunlight. These conditions can warp or degrade the magnetic tape inside, causing loss of video quality or even making the tape unplayable. Avoid storing tapes in basements, attics, or near radiators and windows.

2. Avoid Magnetic Fields

Magnets can erase or distort the recordings on your tapes. Make sure your VHS tapes are kept far from magnets, speakers, or electronic devices that emit strong magnetic fields to protect your videos from damage.

3. Label Your Tapes Clearly

If possible, label each tape with clear, easy-to-read descriptions—such as dates, events, or people featured. This helps us organize your digital files efficiently, making it easier for you to find specific videos later and ensuring your memories stay well organized.

4. Inspect for Damage

Check your tapes for visible damage like cracks, mold, or warping. Moldy or damaged tapes need special attention to avoid further deterioration during the transfer process. If you notice any such issues, please inform us ahead of time so we can prepare the right restoration techniques to recover and preserve your footage.

5. Rewind Tapes Fully

Before dropping off your tapes, rewind them fully to the beginning. This ensures that the entire recording will be captured during the digitization process without missing any important moments.

6. Handle Tapes Carefully

Always handle your VHS tapes gently by their edges and avoid touching the tape itself. Oils and dirt from your fingers can cause playback problems and lower the quality of your videos.

Following these simple tips helps us deliver the best possible digital copy of your treasured VHS memories, preserving them with care and precision for you and your family to enjoy now and in the future.
